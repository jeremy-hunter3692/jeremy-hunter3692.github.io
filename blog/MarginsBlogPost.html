Margins, Padding and Borders

Explanation of what the are and their differences

29 June 2022


CSS Padding, Margins and Borders:

Padding, Margins and borders are methods of creating space around an element with some key differences. Lets start with Margins and padding first. At it's simplest; Margins are a way of creating emtpy space around an element and Padding is a way of creating space inside your element.

It is a lot easier to understand the difference between these ideas when we think about about the element as a block shape rather than say just some text. Take a box shape. Margins will change the space out side of the box, padding will change the space in side of the grey box. Or maybe better to think of it as this box:

If you look at the red line (this is actually the border, which we will come back to but for now we will just use it to locate and speak about the edge.) Margins go from the red line out and Padding goes from the red line in.

Now lets think about it specifcally as some text in a box. In fact specfically all this text in these boxes. We have some white text with a grey background colour. The distance/space between this text and the start of the grey box is the padding. The distance between the edge of that grey box and the black box surrounding it is the margin.

Specifically both Margins, padding and borders add> space to your element. This means that padding will change the size and of your element/block (and therefore sometiems the shape) where as Margins will change the distances between that block/element and another block/element.

Obviously over the top but you can now see that padding adds space to your element. Margins do the same but it is invisible. Look what happens If we create another box the same size as the original pink box but with a 200 pixel Margin.

The text in the pink box is right next to the border and the box is much smaller because there is no padding - the space between the text and the edge/red line. But the distance from the pink box and the grey text boxes above and below has increased because I added Margins on the top and bottom of 200 pixels.

Borders take that edge line which creates space with it. The line itself is bigger. Or that is to say it makes space inbeween the margins and padding. Below is a pink box with much a thicker border.

some text

It's important to note that it makes it's own space and doesn't 'draw' over any of the margin or padding.
That means that if you have a block of 200px with a margin of 200px on one side and then add a border of 10px the total width would be 200 + 200+ 10. The border does not 'draw' over any margins or padding.
