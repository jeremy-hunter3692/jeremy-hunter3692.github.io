Formulate Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal in it’s absolute ideal form is to be able to enter into some freelance web development and/or some remote work. I have many other interests outside of working for income and in and would love to use web development as a way to support that. I would also very happily settle for some steady income.

Since initially applying for the course I’ve found out about creative coding and would love to get into that more. Both for visuals and relating to music. But overall I’m mainly just aiming to be confident in my skills as a coder and be an employable worker so I can choose a healthy place to work with some steady income.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning?

I think my problem solving and general experience of practise and learning music is one of my main strengths. I feel like I can identify the specific skills and pathways needed to accomplish a goal and just sitting down to do the regular work.

Limitations would be perfectionism and obsession with finer (un-important) details.

I can also get a little obsessive with doing things most efficiently - spending an hour learning how to make a half hour task take twenty minutes can be a good thing sometimes (especially while learning) but sometimes a task just needs to be completed rather than done ‘perfectly’. I guess that’s just time/task management.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

While I feel pretty happy with my human skills at the moment and believe it to be one of my strengths more than the technical side am always looking to improve all of these skill across the board, but also to identify and have some more experience in where and how I can apply these in professional setting/in the workplace would be very valuable. More specifically communication is always a particularly helpful skill, I’m always attempting to improve it but being able to ask a question in the best way, communicate my own thoughts logically, clearly and concisely (that last one I probably need to work on the most). That and being able to foster a confidence in my work and place in a team are some of the main skills I would like to see developed.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you:

Putting in two or three good hours every morning helps me assess where I’m at, how much help I’ll need and what and where I need to put my focus on as a preventative measure. If I’m struggling I will make sure I plan in breaks/rest, checking in with my partner and friends. Take a walk, meditate, do some music and if it’s really bad talk to a facilitator for help about how best to approach the situation.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

I will: Post in the discord if I’m struggling to figure out the answer myself and/or can’t google it. While waiting I’ll move on to something else I can work on/manage or take a short break.
